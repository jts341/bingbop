Plateau fix
Diet is your lever, not volume
You're already training enough. The plateau is almost entirely a nutrition problem. Hit 180g protein daily and eat in a slight deficit. The body recomposition will follow.
Progressive overload — track it
Add 2.5kg or 1 rep per set per week on your main lifts. No tracking = no overload = plateau. Use the notes app as a minimum.

Coach guardrails
The 10-minute rule
Commit only to 10 minutes on the main task. No full commitment required — just contact. Momentum takes over after that.
The on-ramp rule
Every session ends by setting up the next one. Open the doc. Write "next step is —". Tomorrow starts already in motion.
The 24-hour re-entry rule
Never more than 24 hours from being back on track. No restarting Monday. No waiting for a clean week. The next day is always re-entry.
The Goggins diagnostic
When the inner critic starts — interrupt it. One question: what specifically made that hard? One sentence. Address that thing. Move on.
The procrastination interrupt
When you catch yourself doing things around the task — name it: "This is the avoidance. The task is that way." Then turn toward it.
Publicist guardrails
One fingerprint per situation
One genuine opinion, one redirect from your own world, or one early exit. Not all three. Consistency over volume.
Cut reactive smiling by half
Replace the reflex smile with a slow nod and held eye contact. Make your warmth earn its place.
Speak to one person in groups
Never address the group diffusely. Pick one person. You create a 1-on-1 inside the group — and you control it.
Exit conversations first
Leave while the energy is still good. People remember the last moment — make it yours.
